WHERE TO INVEST AS STUDENTS?

Now that we have convinced you to start early, let us also tell you about some low-risk and safe investment options for you all. Consider it as your food preferences and then let’s look at what they mean.

  1. Recurring Deposit (RD): Well, this RD is your good ol’ dal chawal! Nothing fancy, Something you cook regularly, will definitely satiate your basic appetite and will not go wrong! It’s safe guys! On a serious note, RD allows you to make regular monthly deposits and earn interest on investment. It is offered by banks. The minimum amount and interest varies from bank to bank. Minimum amount can be as low as Rs. 100 and interest can be from 5% to 8%. The key drawback is that RD is not tax efficient.
  2. Public Provident Fund (PPF): A person should age like fine wine they say, but PPF is that wine you wanna age with. PPF, just like wine, is better when older and if you hurry you won’t get the taste at all. Moreover, this wine gets you tax exemptions! PPF is a long term investment option since it has a lock-in period of 15 years which can be extended for more blocks of 5 years. It is backed by the Government of India, offering safety and full exemption from Tax. The interest rate is determined by the Central Government, revised periodically. The minimum amount is Rs. 500 and the maximum amount is Rs. 1,50,000 per annum.
  3. Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) in Mutual Funds: Well, this is like an assorted box of chocolates which you can’t buy alone with your pocket-money so a group of people contribute to and buy it. However the chocolates you get are in proportion to the money you chip in. You may not always like the chocolates you get in return so there’s some risk involved. This is essentially what mutual funds are.
    SIP allows you to invest regularly a fixed sum (of your choice) in mutual fund schemes. The fixed amount of money can be as low as Rs. 500. SIP is a mode of investment, the risk factor depends on the mutual fund scheme you choose to invest in, hence it is important to thoroughly study the scheme you plan to invest before putting in any money.
  4. Education and Skills: You need to know what to eat when similarly you need to know when to invest in what and how much. Well, continuing our food analogy this isn’t a piece of cake and what you need is proper knowledge, education and skills. As Benjamin Franklin rightly said, An Investment in Knowledge pays the best interest.

HOW TO INVEST AS STUDENTS:

  1. Invest in a medical insurance irrespective of your age.
  2. If you have to keep a certain amount of finances as back-up keep it in a bank as an RD or FD. The money may not grow much but the principal amount will stay safe and intact.
  3. In case of high risk investments like in those of shares, invest in index funds rather than actively managed funds. ‘Index’ are the top 30 companies of the market and investments there have less chances to go wrong.
